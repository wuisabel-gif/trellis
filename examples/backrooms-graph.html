Build your own Obsidian vault

Turn a whole channel into a graph like this in five steps.

  1. Gather the source. Save one text file per page - a Level, Entity, Object, or Group. For a YouTube channel this is TubeScribe; for a wiki like the Backrooms, export or scrape the pages you want.
  2. Make them notes. Obsidian notes are Markdown, so rename each .txt to .md and give it the page's title. A one-line loop does the batch: for f in vault/*.txt; do mv "$f" "${f%.txt}.md"; done.
  3. Open the folder as a vault. In Obsidian choose Open folder as vault and pick vault/.
  4. Connect them. Edges in the graph come from links and tags. Add a topic tag at the top of each note (#level, #entity), or drop a [[Level 0 - The Lobby]] wikilink where one page references another. Every tag and link becomes an edge.
  5. Open Graph View (Cmd/Ctrl + G). Under Groups, color by tag and you get exactly what you see here.

Tip: export as json instead of txt to keep timestamps, so you can deep-link to a moment inside a note.
